You want to learn a language, you need to start from the alphabet. Norwegian uses a modified Latin alphabet, with additions like æ, ø, å. For pronounciation, we need to get familiar with individual letters. 

a {a},  b {be}, c {se}, d {de}, e {ie}, f {eff}, g {ge}
h {ho},  i {i}, j {ye}, k {ko}, l {ell}, m {emm}, n {enn}
o {u},  p {pe}, q {kü}, r {eağr}, s {ess}, t {te}, u {ü}
v {ve},  w {dobbeltve}, x {eks}, y {i}, z {sett}, 
æ {ae}, ø {ö}, å {o}

Pronounciation is mainly rot memorization but after alphabet, there are couple of basic rules that can guide us.

In these notes, margings will mostly contain new words introduced and example sentences. I will follow a formatting rule when giving them. The word or sentence will be bold. English, or Turkish, translation will appear in a list after that. Pronounciation hints will appear in curly brackets. These pronounciation will mostly be taking hints from Turkish because that is where I feel most comfortable, but also from English. Inside regular braces, I will mostly give conjugations. For verbs, conjugation will be in order; imperative, present, past, past participle, present participle, passive. For names, conjugaions also apply since Norwegian has gendered nouns. The order will be singular indefinite, singular definite, plural indefinite, plural definite. An example:

lese: [verb] (les, leser, leste, lest)

  1. to read

{leese}

bok: [substantiv.m] (en bok, boken, bøker, bøkene)

  1. book

{buuk}

Jeg leser en bok. {Yay leeser ien buuk}

If a vowel is followed by two consonants, it is a short vowel. If it is followed by one consonant, then it is a long vowel. 

/a/ {a} \implies Similar to Turkish a
*takk* [/thanks/] {tak.}
*tak*  [/roof/] {taak}

/e/ {ie} \implies Similar to beginning of elevator
*legge* [/to put, to place, koymak/] {legge}
*lege*  [/to heal/] {leege}

/i/ {i} \implies Similar to Turkish i
*finne* [/to find/] {finne}
*fin*  [/fine, ok, good/] {fiin}

/o/ {u} \implies When short, like Turkish o. When long, is like Turkish u.
*komme* [/to come/] {komme}
*skole*  [/school/] {skuule.}

/u/ {ü} \implies Similar to Turkish ü
*ull* [/wool/] {üll}
*ulykke*  [/accident, mishap/] {üülikke}

/y/ {i} \implies A vowel, unlike in Turkish
*lyst* [/lust, desire, pleasure/] {list}
*lys*  [/light, bright, fair, blonde/] {liis}

/ø/ {ö} \implies Similar to Turkish ö
*ørken* [/desert/] {örken}
*øl*  [/beer/] {ööl}

/å/ {o} \implies Similar to Turkish o
*ånd* [/spirit, mind, ghost, geist/] {onn.}
*ål*  [/eel, snake fish thingy/] {ool}

/æ/ {} \implies Like a in apple when short, like a followed by e when long.
*færre* [/fewer, less/] {farre}
*lære*  [/to learn, to teach/] {laere}
